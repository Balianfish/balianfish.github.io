My PhD research is on multiphase flows, which is a type of fluids flow involving two or more fluids. The goal was to develop an accurate and robust numerical method to simulate non-isothermal two-phase flows on an adaptive moving mesh. I have implemented physical models into the existing code and developed a numerical treatment for interface topology changes in the adaptive moving mesh system. These modifications have extended the applicable range of our numerical method. After being thoroughly tested, the method is then used to study binary droplet collision and non-isothermal liquid bridge break-up.

Having spent so many years in the university, I decided to explore how I can levearge my skillsets in the "real world". After I submitted my PhD thesis, I did a six-month internship as a quant analyst in Citi's Central Risk Desk (CRD). CRD manages a portfolio through which all the equity flows within the firm centralise, cross and liduidated back to the market. Using statistically-based alpha and risk models, CRD optimises the portfolio's holdings to maximizee the economic utility for the firm. My main responsibility during the internship was to use Python and kdb+/q for data analysis on CRD's PnL, the toxcity of the equity flows and the execution performance at different venues. I have also re-calibrated the trade volume profile used in the trade impact cost model of CRD's portfolio optimisation tool, and co-built a data analytic service which automatically gather data for CRD's daily work.

My PhD research

My PhD research is on multiphase flows, which is a type of fluids flow involing two or more fluids. The interface separating the fluids is diffcult to deal with as it's a moving boundary with a crucial boundary condition. Our in-house research code tracks the interface explicitly using an adaptive moving mesh and solves the Navier-Stokes equations using finite element method.

During my PhD, I have implemented non-isothermal effects (heat transfer, thermocapillarity and thermo-buoyancy) into the the existing code for isothermal multiphase flows; I have also developed a robust numerical treatment for interface topology changes such that our method can be applied to simulate flows with interface break-up; All modifications have been thouroughly validated through benchmark tests; The validated method is used to study the collision of two droplets and the break-up of a non-isothermal liquid bridge. The results have been summarised into two draft papers, one under review (manuscript) and one under preparation.
